Graphical Overview

Description

This is the overall data model for the project TibSchol - The Dawn of Tibetan Buddhist Scholasticism (11th-13th c.). This project has received funding from the European Research Council (ERC) under the European Union's Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme (grant agreement No 101001002). It is hosted at the Institute for the Cultural and Intellectual History of Asia of the Austrian Academy of Sciences. Note: The description below is a work in progress. The graphical overview represents the relations between entities (Place/Work/Person/Instance). "Excerpts" (in TEI documents) and "External references" operate as Support for these relations.

Classes

Class: "Instance"

Definition

An <INSTANCE> is the particular embodiment of a specific <WORK>.

NOTE 

An <INSTANCE> is notably instantiated in writing in a physical object (manuscript, blockprint, book, etc.). Such resource may or may not be extant, or might be extant but not accessible. In addition to the <WORK>, an instance may include paratextual elements and extra-textual elements.

An <INSTANCE> can be related to another <INSTANCE>; to a <PERSON>; or to a <PLACE>.

Properties

Property Name / Cardinality Data Type Remarks
Attributes
Cardinality: 1		 short text Attributes of <INSTANCE>-entities are: identifier(s) of the instance (e.g., signature on the manuscript, catalogue number, TibSchol number); the title (identical to the name of the <WORK>); the source (bibliographical reference for the instance itself or for a physical or digital copy of the original physical object); external link(s) (e.g., to the online library of the Buddhist Digital Resource Center https://library.bdrc.io/, which provides a link to digital images for numerous items); length (number of folios); indication of proofreading by scribe or reader. Pertinent features of the <INSTANCE> not already included in the set of attribute fields are recorded in the field "Item description," any additional comments in the field "Comments."

Class: "Work"

Definition

The abstract notion of an intellectual creation by one or several <PERSON>s, irrespective of its exact transmitted version, language, form etc.

NOTE 

A <WORK>is transmitted in one or several <INSTANCES> - (see "InstanceToWork") (note: The project does not deal with Instances for Indian works). <WORK> are extant or non-extant. A <WORK> is extant if it has at least one currently extant <INSTANCE>.

A <WORK> can be related to a<PERSON>(see "PersonToWork"); to a <PLACE>; and to another <WORK>.

Properties

Property Name / Cardinality Data Type Remarks
Attributes
Cardinality: 1		 short text Attributes of a <WORK> are its name (title and alternative titles, including in other languages); subject (what the work is about, from a category list); date (the date it was composed, when known, or between the date of the author's birth +10 and the year of the author's date, if author's dates are known); external link(s) (e.g., to BUDA https://library.bdrc.io/, which records additional instances, to rKTs http://www.rkts.org/ for Indian works). A free field for comments records pertinent features of the <WORK> not included in the set attribute fields. For Indian Works (for which Instances are not considered), the reference number of the sDe dge canon is provided.

Relations (outgoing)

  • <Work> commentary on (has as commentary) <Work>
  • <Work> composed at (place of composition of) <Place>

Relations (incoming)

  • <Work> commentary on (has as commentary) <Work>
  • <Instance> instance of (has as an instance) <Work>
  • <Person> is author of (authored by) <Work>

Class: "Place"

Definition

Any kind of location.
NOTE 

The project focuses on real locations situated within the human realm. These are e.g., countries, regions, towns and villages, as well as monasteries.

A <PLACE> can be related to another <PLACE>; to a <WORK> (see "WorkToPlace"); to an <INSTANCE> (see "InstanceToPlace"), or to a <PERSON> (see "PersonToPlace").

Properties

Property Name / Cardinality Data Type Remarks
Attributes
Cardinality: 1		 short text Attributes of a <PLACE> are: name (and alternative names); GPS coordinates; external link. A free field for comments records pertinent features of the <PLACE> not included in the set attribute fields.

Relations (outgoing)

  • <Place> is located within (contains) <Place>

Relations (incoming)

  • <Person> active at (place of activity of) <Place>
  • <Work> composed at (place of composition of) <Place>
  • <Instance> copied/written down at (place of scribing for) <Place>
  • <Place> is located within (contains) <Place>

Class: "Person"

Definition

The notion of Person includes human beings as well as Buddhas and Bodhisattvas.
NOTE 

The project focuses on persons from the 10th to 15th century who were active in scholastic networks in Tibet - the main roles considered being those of authors and teachers/students, but also those of manuscript owners and donors. Also included are persons acting as links between the preceding persons, or whose connection with them allows putting them on the map, independently of their role.

A <PERSON> can be connected to a <PLACE>; a <WORK>, an <INSTANCE> (see "InstanceToPerson"); and to another <PERSON>.

Properties

Property Name / Cardinality Data Type Remarks
Attributes
Cardinality: 1		 short text Attributes of a <PERSON> include: name (the name of the person and alternative names and titles, including in other languages); dates (birth, death, floruit - exact or approximation to be mapped to a date range); type (gender, nationality, monastic/lay status); external link (link to external resources providing additional information about that person, such as BUDA https://library.bdrc.io/). A free field for comments records pertinent features of the <PERSON> not included in the set attribute fields.

Class: "External reference"

Definition

A passage in a historical work or a publication that supports the relation between two entities.

Properties

Property Name / Cardinality Data Type Remarks
Attributes
Cardinality: 1		 short text The attributes of an external reference are its type (Zotero entry or another type of support) and for Zotero entries the Zotero ID of the publication in TibSchol Zotero library in which the passage supporting the relation is found.

Relations (outgoing)

No outgoing relations defined.

Relations (incoming)

No outgoing relations defined.

Class: "Excerpt"

Definition

A textual passage from an <INSTANCE>, as represented in a <TEI DOCUMENT>.
NOTE Excerpts that are thematically relevant or can play the role of support for the relation between entities are marked up with the tag "seg" in the TEI document. Their content is critically edited.

Properties

Property Name / Cardinality Data Type Remarks
Attributes
Cardinality: 1		 short text Attributes of an <EXCERPT> in the <TEI DOCUMENT> include: the identifier; the status (unreviewed to finalized); and optionally a topic (what an excerpt is about - controlled vocabulary).

Relations (outgoing)

  • <Excerpt> is edited and marked up in (contains) <TEI Document>

Relations (incoming)

No outgoing relations defined.
